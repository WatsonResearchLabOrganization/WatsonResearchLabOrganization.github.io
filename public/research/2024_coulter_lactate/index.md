We are developing and validating a noninvasive optical sensor to continuously monitor **blood lactate levels** in patients undergoing cardiac surgery with cardiopulmonary bypass. This work is supported by a **Coulter Translational Research Partnership grant** and conducted in collaboration with UVA Anesthesiology and the UVA Health ICU team.

### Study Overview
- Prospective observational study of **50 adult cardiac surgery patients**
- Lumos sensor applied during surgery and postoperative ICU stay
- Blood lactate measured every 4 hours via standard care
- Lumos spectral readings compared to clinical lactate values
- Goal: Validate real-time trend tracking and cross-skin-tone performance

### Goals
- Enable early detection of sepsis risk and poor perfusion
- Provide a noninvasive, continuous alternative to intermittent lactate blood draws
- Build toward a real-time sepsis alert system using biosensor trends
